FAQ

The questions I keep getting asked on LinkedIn, answered here so you don't have to wait for a reply.

Which PMBOK edition are these mocks based on?▾
These are based on PMBOK 7th edition. The format should still hold up for the current exam - the main changes are case-study style questions and a shift in weightage across the three exam domains (People, Process, Business Environment). The underlying judgement-based question style hasn't changed.
Is this site affiliated with PMI?▾
No. This is an independent, free resource I built for myself while preparing, and I'm sharing it publicly. It's not affiliated with or endorsed by PMI or the Project Management Institute. PMP is a registered mark of PMI.
Do I need my 35 contact hours or PDUs before using this?▾
Yes. This site is only for practice - it doesn't replace your training hours or PDUs. Get those from a PMI-authorized training provider first, submit your application, then come here to test your judgement before exam day.
Is this really free? What's the catch?▾
All three mock exams are completely free, no sign up, no email required. There's also an optional paid 1:1 consultation (₹499) for people who want personal guidance on their application or exam strategy - but the mocks themselves are free for anyone, whether or not you ever book that.
How accurate are the questions? Can I trust the answer key?▾
I built these from my own prep and reviewed them carefully, but they're not officially reviewed by PMI. If you spot something that looks wrong, please use the feedback page to tell me - I fix reported errors directly in the mocks.
Do these count as PDUs or official practice exams?▾
No. These are unofficial practice tools only, meant to sharpen your judgement on exam-style questions. They don't count toward PDUs or any official PMI requirement.
Which mock should I start with?▾
If you're fairly prepared, start with the 121-question mock - it's closest to the real exam length and pacing. If you want to build stamina or drill specific weak areas, the 200-question bank is better for repeated passes. The 100 drag-and-drop set matches PMI's newer interactive question styles.
