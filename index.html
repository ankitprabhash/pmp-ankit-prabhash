First, the mindset

PMP is not a memory test

Most people preparing for PMP think it is about remembering ITTOs, formulas, or 49 processes by heart. That is not the real exam. PMI has moved the exam toward situational judgement - they give you a project scenario and ask what a good PM would do next.

So the actual skill you are building is: read the situation, remove the noise, and pick the answer that protects the project, the team, and the sponsor's interest - in that order, most of the time. This comes only with practice, not with re-reading notes one more time.

What this site is, and is not This is only a practice ground for mock questions. It does not replace your 35 hours of training, your PDUs, or your application to PMI. Do those first with a proper REP. Then use these mocks to test your judgement, not your memory.

Why this is free

I made these for myself first

When I was preparing for my own PMP exam, I looked for free mock tests online. Most of what I found on YouTube were videos five or six hours long. Good content, but hard to sit through in one go, especially on a work night after a full day at office.

So I built my own mock tests instead - using basic Python and some help from AI to put the questions together in a format I could actually use: open the page, answer at my own pace, see my score, move on.

I cleared my PMP. These mocks are exactly what I used. There are already many free options out there, so I am not trying to sell anything here. Just putting mine out too, in case the format works better for someone else the way it worked for me.
