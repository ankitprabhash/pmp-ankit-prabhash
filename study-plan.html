A simple plan

How to actually use these mocks

Not a rigid schedule - a rough shape for your last few weeks before the exam. Adjust the pace to however much time you have left.

WEEK 1

Baseline - find out where you actually stand

Take the 121-question mock cold, no notes, timed if you can manage it. Don't study for it first. The point isn't to score well - it's to find your weak domains honestly.

  • Note which questions you got wrong, not just the score
  • Look for patterns - is it People, Process, or Business Environment questions tripping you up?
WEEK 2

Drill the weak spots

Use the 200-question bank, but don't grind it top to bottom. Focus on the domain that hurt you most in Week 1. Read the explanation for every question you get wrong, right or not - understanding why matters more than the answer.

WEEK 3

Get comfortable with the newer question styles

Run through the 100 drag-and-drop set. PMI's current exam mixes in matching, ordering, and multi-select formats - if your prep so far has only been plain MCQ, this is where that gets fixed.

WEEK 4

Full mock, under real conditions

Retake the 121-question mock, this time timed and without interruptions - phone away, one sitting, like the real thing. Compare this score to Week 1. The gap is your actual progress, not a guess.

  • If you're consistently above your target score with 2+ full run-throughs, you're likely ready
  • If specific domains are still weak, go back to the 200-question bank for those areas only
The one thing that actually matters PMP questions test judgement, not recall. When you get one wrong, don't just memorize the correct option - ask what principle made it correct. That's the thing that transfers to a question you haven't seen before.
